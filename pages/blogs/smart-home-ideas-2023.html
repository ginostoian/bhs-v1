Smart home ideas and trends - 2023. Save time & money

For those who grew up with the dream of flying cars, the reality of the intelligent life we ​​live and surround ourselves with makes that dream seem more and more real. The best examples are working in the background right now and they make our life easier, this is the beauty of modern smart home trends and ideas.

What is a smart home?

If we want to give a rigid and basic definition of a smart house, we can say that it is a residence that uses internet-connected devices to automate and control different aspects of a house, such as security, lighting, temperature and appliances. The benefits of a smart home include increased energy efficiency, improved security, and greater convenience.

In reality, there is no concrete definition, but we can consider a smart home one that uses smart devices to control and automate household functions often through voice commands and mobile apps.

The benefits of such a house are energy efficiency, security, entertainment and comfort, all these benefits helping to save time and money. The top benefit of a smart home is that you can remotely control and monitor home devices.

'Smart technology is constantly evolving to help people live their everyday lives simpler as well as creating new ways to keep our homes safer and easier to control,' says Naeem Adam, tech expert at electronics retailer AO.com.

To understand how trendy these smart homes are and how big they have become since they make our lives much easier, the most accessible example of smart technology that I think most of you have heard of is Amazon’s Alexa which can control smart devices by voice control. They have now connected more than 300 million smart home devices globally, and each one has the capability of controlling so much more.

In the UK, according to research by eco experts, there are around 2.2 million smart homes, all using technology to help owners reduce electricity bills, but also to benefit from the help in simplifying their everyday life.

1. How to Choose your Smart Devices

There are thousands of ways to automate your home, but we suggest starting slowly with one or two smart devices before gradually adding more. There are several factors to consider when you want to buy smart devices for your home like , setup, lifestyle and research.

When it comes to technology, the sky's the limit, both for what they can do, but also for their costs. For this reason, it is very good to know what your limit is and to establish a budget. In addition to the costs, you must also take into account your level of training in terms of smart devices and how familiar you are with them. These are meant to make your life easier, not to complicate it

Before deciding to purchase a smart device, do some research about it, read customer opinions, independent reviews, and buyer guides and always adapt what you buy to your needs and lifestyle. What someone likes and is useful for them, may be useless for you if you don't have similar lifestyles

2. What are the most desired smart devices?

With the rise of the Internet and smartphones, smart homes began to take off, their biggest advantage being that they can be controlled remotely. Today, there are countless smart home devices on the market, from thermostats and security cameras to door locks and light bulbs. We cannot say what would be the best smart device for a home because it depends a lot on the needs of each person and what they want to achieve with that device, but we highlight some products for smart home beginners that are easy to use, affordable, or too good to pass up.

Smart lighting - light bulb

Smart lighting systems are internet-connected devices that allow homeowners to control and automate their home's lights. They can be programmed to turn off lights when they're not needed, such as when everyone has left the house and can even be controlled remotely via smartphone apps.

Smart lighting systems use sensors and algorithms to monitor when lights are needed and when they're not. They can also be controlled remotely via smartphone apps, so you can turn off lights that you left on by accident or turn on lights when you're away from home. Some smart lighting systems also include features such as voice control and the ability to set schedules and scenes, such as "movie mode" or "dinner party mode."

Some popular smart lighting systems include Philips Hue, LIFX, and Cree Connected. These systems typically include a hub that connects to your home's Wi-Fi network and can control multiple light bulbs, allowing you to easily control the lighting in different rooms of your home.

Smart light bulbs are one of the easiest and cheapest ways to start automating your home. Because smart bulbs are LED, they can use anywhere from 75 to 90% less energy than your old bulbs. They also last for up to 25 years, which should save you even more money.

Smart Plugs

Smart plugs are budget-friendly, easy to use and can turn any electronic appliance into a smart device. There are plenty of benefits when using a smart plug, starting from automation to saving energy, to creating a more efficient and safer environment for your home.

After setup, you can schedule lights by using an app to set timers. This device can make it look like you're home, even when you're not or you can set it to work with an automatic timer, which is very useful for example when it comes to holiday decorations so that they automatically turn on when the sun goes down and even turn off when you go to bed.

The possibilities for how to use this function stretch as far as you can be creative.

Smart plugs can be easily used to eliminate wasted energy, so these devices can transform your home not only into a smart one but also into an eco-friendly green home. Smart plugs use about 1-2 Watts of power. Your energy bill is measured in kilowatts per hour. That’s 1,000 Watts to 1 kWh. So, 1 watt of power can translate to 8.76 kWh of electricity over one year which means a lot of potential energy saved and also a decrease in expenses.

Secure your home with Smart Tech

The times when home security was achieved by a very expensive security system or a big dog have passed, and now with a few smart devices, we can protect our home and its surroundings very easily and financially accessible. Smart security systems use cameras and sensors to monitor the interior and exterior of a home. They can also be integrated with other smart home devices, such as door locks and lights, that can notify you of any activity at home or in the garden. Some systems also include features such as facial recognition and the ability to call the police in case of an emergency.

Video doorbells can instantly notify you via your smartphone when someone is approaching your home, even if they don't ring while taking photos and videos.

Smart Locks are very useful for those who wish to arm and disarm their door remotely or to allow access to non-homeowners such as the cleaner or babysitters. Smart locks eliminate the need to hide the key under the doormat which leaves a lot to be desired in terms of home security. These locks may have a built-in keypad that allows you to provide access codes for guests, but they may also offer other methods of locking or unlocking the door such as fingerprint, RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) card and a manual key.

Smart Switches

A smart switch replaces a traditional wall switch and controls the flow of power to a certain area, this means it will control whatever the traditional switch controls. The switch connects to the internet and allows you to control it remotely via an app or a voice assistant like Alexa.

Whether you choose a smart switch or a smart bulb or both, you will experience the best that smart lighting has to offer. The difference between these two smart devices is that a smart switch gives you the ability to make any of your existing fixtures smart, while smart bulbs give you the ability to play with colour.

Smart thermostats – Save energy and cut bills

Smart thermostats are internet-connected devices that allow homeowners to control their home's temperature remotely. They use sensors to monitor the temperature in different parts of the house and can be programmed to adjust the temperature based on your schedule, so they can adjust the temperature accordingly. These thermostats can be controlled via smartphone apps and can integrate with other smart home devices, such as Amazon's Alexa or Google Home.

'Installing a smart thermostat means you can control your heating on the go and avoid wasting money on an empty home,' suggests Gav Murray, Director at Hive. 'If you’re stuck at work or delayed on the way home, you can use your smartphone to stop the heating from coming on too early. It can save you up to £311 off your energy bills a year.'

You can also precisely control your heating schedule room by room by installing smart radiator valves. These can be set to eco or holiday mode which can save you an estimated £575 per year compared to a system without these devices

Health and Wellness Gadgets

In recent years, businesses in health tech have grown, taking care of our health becoming more and more important to us. Many wellness gadgets have been developed that not only take care of the house but also of our health such as smart scales that track our body composition, smartwatches that track blood oxygen and heart rate, posture trackers and blood pressure monitoring monitors.

For those who suffer from allergies, air purifiers are the most recommended. This is an area where smart technology can be very useful and can improve your health at home. The latest connected air purifiers detect levels of particulate matter in the air and adjust the power automatically.

Health also means a good state of mind, and for this reason, devices have been created that also take care of your well-being. An example of this is showers that can be controlled by voice and are associated with lights, music and temperature to create a spa experience right at your home.

Voice Control

Smart home assistants are internet-connected devices that allow homeowners to control other smart home devices, such as lights and thermostats, with just their voice. They can also be used to play music, set timers, and answer questions. Smart home assistants use voice recognition technology to understand and respond to spoken commands.

Smart speakers from Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant and Apple’s Siri, have proven to be spectacularly popular, with 300 million devices sold globally. These devices can be controlled via voice commands, you can listen to music, read the news, initiate video calls and much more.

The success of this voice assistant has led to an increasing number of compatible devices, from TVs, soundbars, laptops and even cars, with hardware built-in.

Smart speakers such as the Google Nest Audio or Echo Dot also act as a bridge to other smart home kits, so if you need to check the video doorbell, close the curtains or dim the lights, all you need to do is ask.

Windows equipped with technology

If until some time ago only in movies and in luxury houses and hotels we saw curtains and blinds that could be controlled by remote control, these days, thanks to smart wireless control it is easier and cheaper to have this luxury right at home.

SwitchBot has a varied range of plug-and-play gadgets to open and close curtains and blinds using smartphones. You can even set timers for sunrise or sunset, a fact that can improve the quality of your sleep, but at the same time has a security role when you are not at home.

'Electric blinds can also help with energy efficiency as they can be programmed to close when the sun disappears and temperatures start to drop, maximising heat retention,' says Lisa Cooper, Head of Product at Thomas Sanderson. 'And of course, they’re perfect for hard-to-reach windows such as increasingly popular gable ends.'

Uses technology to make your kitchen smarter

App-controlled devices at first seemed like a fad and something funny, but we have to admit that remote control has become very useful in our busy and crowded days. Again, technology comes to our aid and makes our life easier and for this reason, the kitchen can be equipped with smart appliances such as fridges that can notify you when you run out of milk or which you can see through a web camera.

There are also hoods that, in addition to having a built-in screen for streaming content, also maintain air quality at home by constantly monitoring air quality and adjusting suction.

What should you add to your smart home?

If you consider that you have enough basic smart devices in your home, but still want to add something more, we recommend that you consider solar panels and energy storage batteries to help manage the spiralling energy costs.

Prices can start from £5,000, being a big investment at the beginning, but according to Project Solar a 3kW -12 panel- system will generate around 3,400kWh of electricity per year, the equivalent to 97,000 hours of fridge usage, 1417 hours of oven usage and 5,000 hours of washing machine usage.

If you want to start with lower costs and familiarize yourself with this solar energy production system, many companies such as Arl, Eufy, Ring and many other smart home security brands sell affordable solar panels to charge lights, cameras and sensors.

Another great way to reduce energy costs is storage batteries. These batteries are charged using portable solar panels and have a capacity that can charge a house full of phones, laptops, tablets and gadgets.

Does a smart home have added value?

According to research from Compare the Market, 51% of Brits are willing to pay up to £2500 more for a home that has smart technology

Relatively, it may seem like a small amount, but a house that uses smart elements and devices will impress potential customers much more and will sell faster. It should be taken into account that even a smart doorbell that does not cost more than £100 can impress, as well as smart LED bulbs can make a very good impression considering the current energy context.

Any technology that can save money can help increase the value of a home

Dare to live smart! Keep up with the world of technology that evolves every day with one goal, to make our lives easier. Take advantage of any gadget that can help you save time and money. Smart homes are becoming more and more popular with the technology that is always evolving and improving. They offer a wide range of benefits, from increased energy efficiency and security to improved convenience and control. By planning and budgeting for a smart home, choosing the right devices, and installing and setting them up, you can enjoy all the benefits that smart home technology has to offer.
