Designing & renovating your small bathroom - advice, tips & tricks. design

If you are looking for small bathroom renovation ideas then you are in the right place. Bathrooms come in all sizes and shapes but the reality is that small bathrooms are one of the most challenging spaces when it comes to decoration. But you can have the desired bathroom because when it is done properly, the whole room can seem brighter and more spacious.

Good interior design has proven to us countless times that size does not matter if the style is chosen with great skill. You just need some inspiration and some tips from the pros to start rethinking your small bath place. Your small bathroom may just be your next favourite room.

Don't let limited space stop you from living large!

How To Make A Small Bathroom Look Bigger

Because we want to make every inch count and make you forget how small your bathroom is, this post also includes tips on how to make a small space look bigger. We want to prove that with some clever design ideas, tiny bathrooms have tons of potential not just to be incredibly functional and charming but super-stylish too.

Until recently, small bathrooms always required a compromise and most of the time we had to give up the bathtub, the first victim in the space battle. Another major problem was represented by the storage spaces, most of the time non-existent.

When it comes to small bathroom design, clever planning, which includes everything from smart storage options to fitting choices, to layout, is key to ensuring that even a tiny space can still feel spacious. Through these small bathroom ideas, we want to go beyond the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãmaking the most of the available space and we want to show that bold design elements fit very well even in tiny rooms and that stylish finishes that are also practical are the icing on the cake.

James Roberts, Director at Sanctuary Bathrooms says, 'If your bathroom is modest in size, there‚Äôs no need to compromise on aesthetics. While there are some limitations design-wise, small bathrooms can still be spectacular by making the most of the space you have with clever storage, layout, and use of colour.'

Small bathroom ideas

From storage solutions to tiling tricks, finding the best bathroom ideas to suit your space is not easy, but we have collected the most ingenious ideas and solutions so that limited space does not stop you from living large

A couple of space-saving tips mixed in with a splash of gorgeous accessories and modern furniture will turn the most modest of bathrooms into an enviable space.

Tips and Tricks

Read on to find out how to create and design the small bathroom of your dreams.

Choose A Light Color Scheme

Most of the time light colours make a space feel bigger. If you have difficulties in choosing a design to decorate a small bathroom, just keep it simple with natural and soft shades. Opt for accessories and furniture with clean lines and keep the room as clutter-free as possible to create the illusion of space.

Ruth Foster, Interior Designer at Victoria Plum says, 'Neutral d√©cor in the bathroom has always been popular, however even more so in smaller spaces. Adopting light shades like taupe, cream and white will create a lighter and brighter bathroom, making it appear larger than it is. If you keep this in mind when choosing the colour of your wall and floor tiles, you can create a warm and harmonious design with timeless elegance.'

Use Mirrors

Mirrors always make a room feel bigger by creating the illusion of spaciousness. You will be surprised how much a mirror can open up a room, especially if your bathroom is not only small but also dark. A wall-to-wall mirror will bounce the natural light around the room and make the whole space feel bigger.

A mirror placed in the right place can do wonders in a room, that's why it's the favourite trick used by interior stylists to make a room feel larger.

Glass Shower Door

Skip the dark curtain or foggy glass, and opt for glass doors - these will make the space bigger and let the light into the room.

Walk In Shower

A stylish option for bathroom design is a walk-in shower which is ideal for small bathrooms because it helps to create space and offers more flexibility for layout.

You don't have to worry about the space of the shower door, with a walk-in shower you won‚Äôt have that problem. Find more information about this trend in a previous article about the top bathroom design trends in 2023.

Shower Bath

A small space requires us to be creative and find the best options for our desires.

Lee Reed, Head of Design at Easy Bathrooms says, 'The choice of shower that you decide on can be partly dictated by the space and shape of your bathroom. If space is limited, having a shower over the bath is a practical way of ensuring you can still have both, which can be important in a family bathroom. If you are going for this option, specially designed baths (L or P shaped so that they provide a larger area when showering) can work.'

'However, you don‚Äôt have to give up the dream of a freestanding bath/shower mix either. Traditional models such as boat baths can be a great option when used with either a riser kit or a ceiling-mounted shower. To stop water splashing everywhere, team it with an oval shower ring and shower curtain.'

Opt for an Oval Bath

Be bold with your ideas and don't be afraid to include a freestanding bath in your small bathroom. Choose an oval bath to cut corners and boost the illusion of space. It will create a strong visual focal point giving the space an intimate and comfortable style. Even if it will not be as spacious as a traditional bathtub, a tinier tub is still a wonderful alternative.

Barrie Cutchie, Design Director at BC Designs says, 'Even in small bathrooms, many people aspire to a freestanding bath to create plenty of wow and a focal point that instantly makes the room seem more luxurious. However, budgets and space constraints mean this isn‚Äôt always an option, yet a standard fitted bath can be just as interesting when it comes to bathroom design, particularly if you decide to dress it up with tiles.'

Multifunctional & Floating Furniture

Floating and multifunctional furniture is always a must in small spaces. Floating furniture opens up the floor and will give a bathroom a more spacious feel.

Choose a beautiful bathroom vanity and use the space under the sink to store towels or products that you don‚Äôt always need. Also, you can install a tower rack hanging underneath the sink. A floating shelf will be a lifesaver for essentials like hand soap, tissues and candles

Don't forget to use the space above the toilet.

The power of natural light and a good lighting installation

Natural light is one of our favourite tricks when we want to give the feeling of more space. In a small room, natural light is too important to block it with curtains. If the space allows you to keep the windows bare then feel free to do this.

Also, a well-thought-out lighting installation can give the illusion of a generous and welcoming space. Lights play an essential role in the atmosphere of a room.

Add A Skylight

Skylights are a great way to bring more natural light into your tiny bathroom. If you can't have a normal window or for privacy reasons, skylights are a wonderful option

Go Vertical

One of the best tips in any small room is to think vertically. A smart way in making a bathroom look bigger is by adding vertical wall tiles. In this way, your small bathroom will look higher and therefore bigger. Also, hang art vertically to get an elegant and unique style

Go for Terrazzo or Trick with Tiles

The dimensions of the small bathroom space you have and what the eye perceives are not necessarily the same thing, due to the tricks we can use to fool the true dimensions. Play with the boundaries of perception by matching the wall with the floor to double the impact, tiling with the same design will make it harder to distinguish where each begins, thus making the space feel larger.

In a previous article, we talked about the Terrazzo style and how attractive this design is through the visual play it offers.

Dare to go Dark and Moody

Normally, in small spaces it is recommended to use light colours, white being everyone's favourite when it comes to creating the illusion of a larger space, however, we can't deny how fabulous and trendy darker paint shades can be.

Black gives depth and creates an illusion of a larger space and also will draw attention to the light fixtures, for this reason, we must be very careful how we illuminate such a room, light being the star.

James Roberts at Sanctuary Bathrooms says, 'Although it might seem a bad idea to use dark colours in a small space, there are ways to implement it without it looking gloomy! If you want to go dark on the walls, add pops of light colours through furniture and fittings. With botanicals set to be big in 2023, try a dark green with some accessorising plants in various shades to add dimension.'

Wet Room

Many times, in small bathrooms we encounter problems when we want to install a shower and a separate shower can often be considered a luxury if space is tight.

However, there are a couple of clever ideas like wet rooms and folding shower screens that take up much less room than a typical shower. For these, there is no need for bulky shower trays and can easily blend into the aesthetics of the rest of the room.

A stylish wet room idea is a great way to make the most out of a small room and when it is beautifully finished it can be stunning.

How do you design a small bathroom?

Consistency is key. Use the same type of tiles for both the wall and the floor, and choose glass shower doors ‚Äî or even no shower doors to keep it feeling open and breezy. Don't forget to use every element to the maximum, choosing multifunctional furniture and accessories to store as many things as possible. Visually play with things vertically or even by choosing a bold and playful wallpaper

What is the best colour for a small bathroom?

How should I layout a small bathroom?

Ed Norris, National Sales Development Manager at Mereway Bathrooms says, 'A common issue with smaller bathrooms is the lack of space for toiletries and fresh towels. Utilise the storage around your sanitaryware; cupboards will keep things neatly tucked away but remember to introduce a segment of open shelving too to add a visual relief that still provides a place to put things but helps the space feel airier. Keeping storage all within one area will bring a focus to the layout and let you move around more freely.'

James Roberts, Director at Sanctuary Bathrooms, adds, 'Consider going wall-hung with your fittings and fixtures.
