How much does a bathroom renovation cost in 2022?

Planning a bathroom renovation and wondering how much it will end up costing? We try and breakdown the costs of a bathroom renovation as much as possible so you know how to budget your home improvement project.

First of all, why should you renovate your bathroom?

A bathroom refurbishment is a big project to take on but it also comes with a lot of benefits. There are plenty of reasons to want to renovate a bathroom but we will list only a couple of them:

  1. The bathroom is old and shows signs of wear. In this situation you are refurbishing the bathroom for you and your family. The bathroom is the most used room in your home so you want it to be comfortable, good looking and practical.
  2. You‚Äôve just purchased your home and want to put your personal touch on it‚Äôs design. When purchasing a new house, it‚Äôs usually done the way the former owner liked and this, most of the time, does not match with your style and taste. Modernising the bathroom of your new house will make it feel more like home.
  3. You are thinking of your home‚Äôs value. Bathroom renovations have a very good return on investment and will increase your home‚Äôs valuation if you are thinking of selling or remortgaging.
  4. There are more reasons to renovate your bathroom, including leaky and potentially dangerous plumbing and electrics, carpeted flooring and more ‚Äúbad design and practical choices‚Äù.

Bathroom renovation cost and factors that influence it

The cost for labour and construction materials for a bathroom renovation is between ¬£4,500 and ¬£7,500.

There are a lot of factors to take into consideration and decisions to be made when quoting for a bathroom renovation project. Each of them will influence this cost, with some projects going even higher than the above mentioned threshold because of their complexity. Let‚Äôs take a look at these below:

1. The size of the bathroom.
We usually characterise bathrooms into small, medium and large bathrooms. The bigger the space, the more materials and time will go into renovating it thus the price will be higher.

2. The layout of the bathroom
You have 2 choices here. Keep the layout as it is or change it by moving the position of the bathroom fixtures around the bathroom to better manage the space.

The first option will obviously be cheaper as it is less time consuming. There will still be a need to adjust the current plumbing for the new bathroom fixtures but this is less intensive labour. Keeping the same layout is usually a good way to take down the cost of your bathroom refurbishment.

The second option will take the price up as moving the plumbing for the fixtures in different positions is difficult work that requires more time and materials. If your budget allows it, changing the layout of your bathroom is beneficial as it allows you to personalise the space and utilise it more effectively.

3. Bathroom tiling
The way you decide to tile your new bathroom is another big factor that drives the price of your renovation up or down.

Since the bathroom is a wet room, you will need to tile certain spaces, usually those in direct contact with water (i.e. shower enclosure, bathroom enclosure etc.) and the floor.

Depending on preference, you can opt for:

  • Full height tiling in the whole bathroom
  • Full height tiling in the bath / shower enclosure and halfway on the rest of the walls
  • Full height in the bath / shower enclosure and splash back tiling or just decorate the rest of the room

The more tiling you do, the more time it will take and so the more the cost of the bathroom renovation will go up.

Another important fact to take into consideration when choosing your bathroom tiles is, tile size and pattern.

There are tons of tile sizes and even more shapes and patterns you can opt for. But you have to know that the easiest and cheapest option when tiling is to go for simple, big ceramic tiles. These are easy to install and can look great if chosen to match your bathroom fixtures.

Although using small mosaic tiles, hexagonal tiles, weird and complicated patterns or materials ( cement tiles ) can make your bathroom stand out, this will definitely add to the cost of your bathroom refurbishment.

4. Bathroom electrics
In terms of electrics in a bathroom you have to consider the following:

  • Lighting ( wall mounted lights, ceiling light or spotlights )
  • Extractor fan
  • Shaver socket
  • Mains powered mirrored cabinet with LED lighting with optional anti fog pads and sometimes even wireless charging.

On the low end of the cost, you will most likely have included the replacement of the existing light or spotlights and the replacement of the extractor fan.

However, if any of the items above are not already wired in the bathroom and you‚Äôd like to add them, this will increase the overall labour cost.

Please take into consideration that you should only use electrical fittings that are IP 65 ( waterproof rating ).

5. Painting and decorating
When refurbishing your bathroom, you will need to at least paint the ceiling if you opt for the full height tiling option that we discussed about above. If going with a different tiling option, there will be walls to paint as well.

Depending on the state of the walls and ceiling, just filling in small cracks is usually already included in the cost, but there are situations when the walls and/or the ceiling are seriously cracked and also need to be plastered or plaster-boarded in order to obtain a high quality finish.

As you can imagine, the need to plasterboard and plaster will drive the cost up.

All of the above points will be looked at and taken into consideration when the estimator will visit the site in order to gather as much information about the project as he can.

We, at Better Homes Studio, try to offer our clients transparent and detailed quotes, so you can expect to see all of this information in the quote once you receive it.

Bathroom fixtures and tiles

The first major cost of a bathroom renovation is the labour, which we discussed about above. The second is purchasing your new bathroom suite and tiles.

Bathroom suites vary greatly in price depending on style, brand and colour ( chrome, black or brass are the most common ones ).

You also have quality ranges from low end to super luxury with prices from ¬£450 to outrageously expensive.

  • Low end bathroom suite - starting from ¬£450
  • Mid range bathroom suite - starting from ¬£1,800
  • Luxury bathroom suite - starting from ¬£4,000 going to more than ¬£30,000

Choosing the right bathroom suite is really important for both your budget and for the life span of your bathroom.

Although you can get full bathroom suites from as little as ¬£450, we generally don‚Äôt recommend them as they are not great quality and you risk having them fail not long after you‚Äôve purchased them.

For most clients, we recommend getting a mid range bathroom suite.

Conclusion

The cost of your bathroom renovation project is comprised of 2 main items:

  1. Labour
  2. Bathroom suite and tiles

For labour, you can expect to pay anywhere between ¬£4,500 and ¬£7,500, the price being influenced by different choices you make when planning your new bathroom.

The above is the median cost you can expect to pay for a bathroom renovation but this can vary depending on your location, the companies reputation, time of year etc.
