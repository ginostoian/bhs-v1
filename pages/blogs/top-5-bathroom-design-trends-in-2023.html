Top 5 Bathroom Design Trend for 2023

Looking for new bathroom design ideas for 2023? Whether you’re in the middle of a new build project or if you plan to renovate your home’s bathroom, the year 2023 comes with endless ideas. Going beyond spaces for showering or bathing, bathrooms are certainly among the most used rooms in any home and because of this, the bathroom has transformed into a space focused on relaxation, health and wellness. This space has become our stylish sanctuary for all-important mindfulness and self-care and for this reason, we started to give as much attention to it as we do to the main rooms of the house, after all – a breathtaking space can give you a little boost in the mornings and a moment of peace in the evenings The following bathroom trends for 2023 apply to a multitude of design styles and can work with a variety of bathroom sizes. No matter if you love contemporary, minimalist or glamorous interiors, there’s sure to be something to suit your tastes – you only need to discover it!

These are going to be the five biggest bathroom trends for 2023, according to bathroom designers / interior designers.

Spa inspired bathroom design - an oasis of calm in your bathroom

In recent years, many of us have realised the untapped potential of our bathrooms as wellness spaces, and this has led several designers to consider that a top trend for 2023 will be turning the bathroom into the ultimate retreat. Seeing the bathroom as the ultimate retreat, 2023 bathroom design is all about personalization, consider them as spaces in which to take care of your body and relax your mind.

There is an ever-changing mindset when it comes to bathroom design; instead of seeing bathrooms as a place with clear tasks, clients are looking to create lounge spaces where freestanding bathtubs are surrounded by tables for candles and glasses of wine alongside comfortable seating, establishing the bathroom as more than just a place to primp and wash up and for this reason, the spa has become a main inspirational motif.

Steam showers are becoming more and more popular and benches are often requested in bathrooms in order to encourage relaxation and create a luxurious statement. Also, open shelves where you can roll towels as you would see at a luxury spa, and natural and dimmable lighting with soft inviting colours to help it feel zen, are similar spa-inspired trends. All these elements from the world of hospitality combine to create a sumptuous private space for the homeowner to escape at the end of a long day.

Get inspired by Terrazzo

Terrazzo is among the top bathroom tile trends of 2023 having an impressive comeback. Terrazzo decor is currently having a resurgence, it has a timeless finish that's been around for centuries. Apart from being available in almost every colour and size, each terrazzo style is unique, all being equally attractive and also very sustainable.

'We love working with terrazzo and one of its main benefits is just how flexible it can be,’ says Nicoline Sandwith, director of Studio Ogee.

Its characteristic flecks give a sense of retro style to contemporary bathrooms, meaning that you can add a unique pop even if a full bathroom renovation isn’t on your list. This style works on so many levels in terms of providing the colour, pattern and a sense of movement and dynamism - to get all this you can match the wall with the floor to double the impact If you are looking for interesting bathroom ideas without being overpowering, terrazzo is for sure a great choice. This material brings colour, texture and individuality to a bathroom – while being incredibly practical and hardwearing. For example, in a long and narrow bathroom, you can create by using the right tiles, a focal point that draws the length of the room, while its light colours and subtle pattern enlarge the space

It's important to note that terrazzo is a good conductor of heat, so it works well with underfloor heating, which will help keep the cold away and keep the floor dry.

Industrial inspiration

The bathroom trends of 2023 propose we embrace the industrial style and enjoy a bit of steampunk flair and, instead of minimising or hiding the presence of pipework, turn them into a feature. Industrial bathroom design has a focus on strong and tough materials. A bathroom that follows this design has less sleek chrome, and more brass, steel or copper. If you're thinking of including any other materials, aged wood with a slightly tarnished look is a good idea.

The industrial style has its correspondent in the well-known image of New York lofts and to have a bathroom with this design, a key element to have is a bare brick wall.

We recommend having only one wall with exposed brick in your bathroom so as not to load too much visually.

Dark grey tones are the specific colours of this style. We recommend a black frame shower screen and grey or black accessories. The industrial bathroom is all about moving away from elegant and fancy furniture and embracing a slightly more chaotic and rustic design style. One easy way to get this look is by fitting open shelving to hold all your bathroom essentials.

Wood accents - a mix of old and new elements

Many designers noted that bathrooms are finally getting that mix of old and new to create a more inviting, warmer and cosy space that is comfortable enough to spend an hour or two in at a time “We are seeing some interesting trends in bathroom design lately, especially when it comes to incorporating antique furniture in lieu of millwork, and we are also installing art above bathroom vanities, leaving mirrors to be placed in more creative ways,” says New York-based designer David Frazier.

If you are about to refurbish your bathroom, you have to know that wood is becoming a popular material, from flooring to open shelving and cabinetry. Also, there is a great interest in vintage items and stone sinks to give the bathroom an accent of beautifully aged jewellery and all these put together create a one-of-a-kind space.

If this bathroom design caught your attention and suits your style, you must know that the trend uses three or four different materials to create a layered space that looks as if it has evolved over time. In 2023, expect to see a lot more natural wood on bathroom vanities. This increased interest in wooden elements lead to the rapid growth of modern organic style and warm colours that we have seen in the last couple of years.

Wet rooms or walk-in showers?

It seems that 2023 is the year of the shower. Designers agreed and identified that the trends for this year are heading towards an elevated shower routine. With increasingly busy lives and convenience as a priority, we're opting for showers over baths and because of this, we are starting to give them the attention that they deserve.

This being said, what do you prefer - wet rooms or walk-in showers?

The difference between a wet room and a walk-in shower is that in a wet room, the space is completely open plan and the water flows away through a drain in the floor, while a walk-in shower differs simply because of the inclusion of a low profile shower tray and typically has one or two glass screens. Wet rooms and walk-in showers offer a great alternative to the traditional bathroom layout and the costs vary depending on your needs. Both are ideal for small bathrooms, help to create space and offer more flexibility for layout. Compared to traditional shower cabins, you can expand as much as you need to create ample showering space.

Offering a contemporary and stylish option for bathroom design, both wet rooms and walk-in showers exude luxury and can positively impact the value of your property. The minimal fittings mean low maintenance and durability.
