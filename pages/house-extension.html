What we do

We build the right extension for you.

Single storey extension

The addition of single storey extension can add significant value to your home.

Single storey house extensions are built onto a part of the house whereby one or more sides of the extension is attached to the property, and there is only the ground floor plus any basement built to it.

Single storey extensions require building control, but they might not need planning permission.

Let's discuss your project

Side return extension

Perfect for a bit of additional space without causing too much disruption .

Side return extension functions similarly to a rear extension, with the difference being that rather than extending do the rear, the property is extended to the side, making use of the side return, or small ‘alleyway’ between yours and your neighbour’s property.

Simplicity and cost are again the main selling points here, with the side return extension being relatively easy to build, taking less time than some of the other options and consequently also being among the least expensive of all options.

Let's discuss your project

Double storey extension

Need to add more than one room to your home? a double-storey extension is a cost-effective option.

With this type of extension, you could create a large kitchen with a dining area and easy access to the garden on the lower half. Have an extra bedroom, large family bathroom, office or even a home gym on the second half.

Double storey home extensions can be built onto the back or the side of the house.

Let's discuss your project

Over structure extension

An extension over the top of an existing structure such as a garage.

Many UK properties (detached & semi-detached) built within the last 40 years, usually have a single storey side garage. This creates an opportunity for an additional extension on top of the garage.

This type of extension on top of the garage offers significant benefits. Compared to other extension types such as creating a large master bedroom with en-suite.

Let's discuss your project

Basement extension

Usually used to add a gym or game room to your home.

If your property sits on a slope, you could build a semi-basement with a part of the area sitting below the ground. Bringing light and air into half basements is usually straightforward.

f you are adding a side or rear house extension to your house, you might be able to incorporate a basement extension into the design.

Let's discuss your project

Loft conversion

Create an extra room or more by simply converting your loft.

f you have an attic with sufficient floor to ceiling heights, you could create an extra room by simply converting your loft by adding a couple of Velux or other good quality windows to your roof, creating a roof light loft conversion.

f your attic is not high enough, you could still add a room by building a dormer loft conversion or a mansard extension

What is the cost of building a house extension in London?

A house extension cost will depend on the quality of the finish and the materials used. While basic extensions in London can be as cheap as £2000 per square metre, if you want to build a top quality high end extension, the cost can go up to £5000 per sq. m.

How much value will adding an extension add to my home?

High-quality home extensions can increase the value of your London property by up to 25 per cent, which exceeds the expense.

How long does it take to build a home or kitchen extension?

The duration of a home renovation varies greatly dependant on the scope of the work. A small one storey rear or side extension can be completed within three or four months. Wrap-around extensions and larger rear and side return extensions will take longer to build. A double-storey extension might take up to nine months to complete. What are the benefits of extending my London home?

House extensions are growing in popularity as house renovation projects due to the high demand for larger homes. It is an innovative and affordable construction method for specialist builders and the homeowners who decide to add extra space to their property. The different types of house extensions can get you many benefits. As well as the obvious advantages for you and your family of an extension, such as extra space to co-habit in, it can also add light to your London home, making it look brighter and welcoming.

The most common and popular uses for house extensions in London are

Extending the back of the property into an open plan kitchen and dining area while utilising garden space by bringing the outdoors in. Creating an additional bedroom, games room, playroom, home office or gym.
